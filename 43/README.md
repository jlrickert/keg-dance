# On1 Salsa (on1-salsa)

On1 salsa, also known as LA-style salsa, is a style of salsa dancing where
dancers break on the first count of the musical measure. This timing
distinguishes it from on2 (New York-style) salsa and makes it one of the most
popular and accessible forms of salsa dancing worldwide.

## Key Characteristics

**Timing and Basic Step**: In on1 salsa, dancers move on beats 1-2-3 and 5-6-7
of an 8-count measure. The basic step follows a quick-quick-slow,
quick-quick-slow rhythm pattern. This timing is generally considered easier for
beginners to learn compared to on2 timing.

**Linear Dance Style**: On1 salsa is danced in a slot, meaning dancers move back
and forth along a linear path rather than in a circular fashion. This contrasts
with Cuban and Colombian salsa styles, which feature more circular movement
patterns.

**Popularity**: On1 salsa is the most popular form of salsa dancing globally,
likely because the timing is more intuitive for new dancers and the style is
versatile and accessible.

## Comparison with Other Styles

On1 dancing differs from on2 (New York-style) in timing and musical alignment.
More experienced dancers often prefer on2 because it aligns more closely with
salsa music's clave and tumbao rhythms. However, most on2 dancers also know on1,
making LA-style salsa versatile.

On1 shares the early-count break with Cuban salsa but differs in movement
quality. Cuban salsa uses circular patterns and incorporates more Afro-Cuban
movements and hand tricks, while on1 maintains linear slot-based movement.

## Historical Context

LA-style salsa was popularized by the Vazquez Brothers (Luiz, Francisco, and
Johnny Vazquez) in Los Angeles, initially known for flashy, showy moves and big
tricks. Today, the term "LA-style salsa" is used synonymously with "on1 salsa,"
focusing on timing rather than specific styling conventions.
