# Kizomba (kizomba)

Kizomba is an Angolan partner dance that emerged in the late 1970s and 1980s, blending Semba with Caribbean zouk
influences. It is danced in close embrace with a smooth, grounded connection and emphasizes lead-follow communication
through the body rather than hand signals.

## Key characteristics

- Close embrace; chest-to-chest connection drives communication
- Slow, smooth, grounded movement; no bouncing
- 4-count structure; walks, side steps, and saidas (exits) form the vocabulary
- Often danced to kizomba music or ghetto zouk; tempo is slower than salsa or bachata
